7. **Niche Risks**
   Identify the **most significant risks** that could undermine a product or business built in this niche, based on signals from TikTok trend data and content behavior. Base conclusions on observable patterns, not assumptions.

   For each risk, provide:

   * **Risk**: Short label (e.g., "Trend already peaked", "Low purchase intent", "Platform instability")
   * **Signal**: Specific evidence from TikTok — hashtag trajectory, content saturation, engagement-to-conversion gap, creator behavior — that indicates this risk is real
   * **Severity**: Low / Medium / High
   * **Mitigation angle**: Is there a content angle, sub-niche, or product positioning that reduces exposure to this risk?

   Risk types to consider:
   * **Trend reversal velocity** — TikTok trends can peak and collapse within weeks. Are there signs this niche has already passed its inflection point? (plateau in hashtag growth, declining view counts on new content, ironic or backlash content emerging)
   * **Low purchase intent signal** — is engagement driven by entertainment rather than problem-solving? High views with no product links, affiliate traffic, or app downloads in the niche suggest audiences watch but don't buy
   * **Platform instability risk** — TikTok itself faces regulatory uncertainty in key markets. Is the niche's demand primarily TikTok-native, or does it cross to other platforms?
   * **Creator fatigue** — is the niche oversaturated with creators covering the same content, making it hard for a new brand or product to cut through organically?
   * **Misinformation-driven demand** — is the trend fueled by health claims, life hacks, or viral misinformation that could be debunked or regulated, collapsing demand quickly?
   * **Demographic mismatch** — is TikTok engagement concentrated in demographics with low spending power or low willingness to pay for apps (e.g., Gen Z audiences for premium-priced tools)?
